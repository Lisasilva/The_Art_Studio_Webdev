An artists process can often be just as interesting and certainly as valuable, especially in sweat equity, as the finished piece. @justinmzielke is a sculptor who works in traditional mediums, began documenting his process of hand sculpting and kneeding clay by shooting multiple photographs during the process and stitching those photos together.

Chloe Chiasson addresses societal expectations of gender sameness in her enormous mixed media collages. This piece, "Target Practice", is 10' wide and made of oil, acrylic, resin, wool, denim, aluminum, lantern, wood, nails, hot glue, graphite, glitter, LEDs, cigarette butts, matches, bobby pin, sticky note, page from book, washers, ink on shaped canvas.

"World without end/STOP/Gambel's Quail", Oil on salvaged metal Road sign, 30 x 30". Dianne Bennett’s current series of reverential portraits of birds and other wild beings are painted on salvaged road signs celebrating and advocating for the diversity of flora, fauna, and unspoiled terrain native to the Mojave desert.
